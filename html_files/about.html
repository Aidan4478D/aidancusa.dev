About Me

My name's Aidan, and I'm an upcoming college sophomore at The Cooper Union majoring in electrical engineering! I grew up in Monroe, Connecticut and attended Masuk High School where I was an active member of the VEX Robotics team 4478D. Currently, I'm engaged in low-level programming for the Cooper Union's Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition (IGVC) Team.

The Cooper Union

The Cooper Union for the Advancement of Science and Art (The Cooper Union) is a private institute located within the East Village in New York City. The school was founded in 1859 by Peter Cooper and offers degrees in engineering, art, and architecture. Click here to learn more about The Cooper Union!

At The Cooper Union, I've learned to enjoy the world of electrical engineering more than I would have ever thought and have ultimately become fascinated with the subject. I currently have a major GPA of 4.00, an overall GPA of 3.7, and am on a track to graduate with a minor in computer science.

In additon to my coursework, I'm a member of Cooper Union's Intelligent Ground Vehicle Compeition (IGVC) Team. IGVC is an annual international robotics competition for teams of undergraduate and graduate students who design and build an autonomous ground vehicle capable of completing several difficult challenges. I specificlly work in the Drive-By-Wire subteam, and worked on the car's battery monitor sensor firmware which, recieves and decodes the car battery's raw voltage data then sends it on the car's CAN bus system.
